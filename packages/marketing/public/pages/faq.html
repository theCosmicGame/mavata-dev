FAQ

What is Mavata?

Mavata is a subscription platform that helps private companies maximize shareholder value.

Background: It's no secret that most private companies don't know their market value, or what information will attract a premium price from potential buyers.

And as it turns out – many companies end up selling undervalue and paying too much in fees to M&A advisors who capitalize on this information gap.

How we help: We identify & centralize the data that will drive premium value when you sell your business.

We also provide monthly board reports and ongoing valuation monitoring, so you can stay informed and make confident decisions about your company’s future.

What we also do: We enable a private line of communication with buyers at any time. We can also recommend M&A advisors and help vet their fees.

The bottom line: Our goal for you is simple – we want to make sure your business sells for maximum value and avoids paying excessive fees.

Our goal for the world is big – we want to make the capital markets more efficient, transparent, and accessible for private companies.

How much shareholder value can Mavata add?

Mavata believes in transparency.

‍With that in mind: It's hard to estimate potential valuation gain without knowing the current state of your data. Furthermore, there is no fee transparency in the M&A industry – unlike real estate brokers who have standard commission rates, M&A advisors never advertise fees and can charge vastly different commissions for similar transactions. This makes it challenging to estimate exact advisor fee savings from our service.

Ok, but…: We would be disappointed if a typical client did not earn at least a 200% return on Mavata. Frankly, we hope that most clients will do far better. We're happy to share our math with you - just ask.

‍A caveat: Mavata identifies and centralizes key data but relies on clients to provide the underlying data from their businesses. Mavata provides a monthly “Mavata Scorecard” which tracks and rates the availability and quality of your underlying data. Results will vary based on this availability and quality.

When should I start using Mavata?

Mavata can help companies to ramp-up in a pinch or to understand and grow value over years.

For best results, we encourage clients to onboard months or years before considering a sale.

Does Mavata capture all the information required to sell my company?

Typical company sale processes have two stages of due diligence: (1) business due diligence and (2) confirmatory due diligence.

‍Where we focus: Mavata focuses on identifying and centralizing information which helps to maximize shareholder value.  This information is most powerful at the business due diligence stage, when price and economic deal terms are generally agreed.

‍What else is there: Following business due diligence, buyers and their subject-matter advisors conduct a more exhaustive review of books, records, contracts, and operations to confirm there are no red flags. Mavata does not capture the entirety of this information.

Is Mavata an M&A broker or advisor?

No, Mavata is not an M&A broker or advisor.

‍Why this matters: We do not care when or if you sell. We just want to make sure that you are constantly prepared and consistently informed, and properly equipped to maximize shareholder value.

‍Anything else: At your request, we can even recommend good M&A advisors with fair fee structures and help you connect with their prior clients for unvarnished feedback. We can also help you ensure reasonable fees.

Where did the name Mavata come from?

Good question! Mavata is a portmanteau of “Maximum Value Data.”
